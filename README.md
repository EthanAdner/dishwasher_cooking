### Steak sous-vide in a dishwasher

It is my currently unsubstantiated belief that it is possible to cook a steak perfectly (medium rare) in any dishwasher.
I think that by controlling the starting temperature of the steak, and how much thermal insulation surrounds it, you can "tune" a steak to the temperature profile created during the run of a dishwasher.

Why? I am bored, but also I have made this assertion many times and I should finally put my money where my mouth is.

To accomplish this, I am going to do the following:
- characterize the temperature curve of my dishwashers run cycles
- create a solver which attempts to calculate the amount of thermal insulation on the steak, and its initial temperature given a dishwashers thermal cycle.
- show in simulation that such a combination of initial starting temperatures and insulation exists for the profile of my dishwasher
- cook a steak sous vide and enjoy it

The benefits of figuring this out are that everytime you run a dishwashing load, you will essentially get a cooked steak for "free" which you can then sear in a pan.

This probably only works for dishwashers that have non-automatic cycles, meaning cycles that are strictly based on a preset timer and not on some sensing of the water quality.

I will add additional constraints to another document, but I have put forth my hypothesis, and I hope to find evidence that supports it.
